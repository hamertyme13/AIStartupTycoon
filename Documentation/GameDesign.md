# Tech Empire Game Design

## Vision

Tech Empire is a compact management sim about building an AI company under pressure. The player balances cash, research, products, employees, investors, customer trust, and competitors while trying to reach a meaningful exit or long-term dominance.

The game should feel like a living startup boardroom: every month should create a new reason to make a tradeoff.

## Core Loop

1. Review company health, cash, runway, satisfaction, and CEO briefing.
2. Spend money on products, hiring, research, customer success, marketing, or offices.
3. Let time advance and watch revenue, churn, competitors, research, and market conditions update.
4. Respond to events, investor opportunities, customer pressure, and competitor moves.

## 2D HQ Layer

Tech Empire should feel like a living company, not only a dashboard. The HQ scene is the first 2D layer: employees appear as animated avatars inside department zones, and the player can tap them to inspect morale, burnout, loyalty, level, skill, and current assignment.

The 2D layer should remain connected to the simulation model. Employee department changes, firing, morale shifts, burnout, loyalty, and company health should be visible in the office scene without creating a separate parallel game state.
5. Build toward a victory route or recover from a failure spiral.

## Primary Systems

- Products generate customers, revenue, valuation, and market share.
- Employees contribute through departments: Engineering, Research, Product, and Growth.
- Research unlocks technologies and future products.
- AI models provide revenue, valuation, and market-share boosts.
- Investors add cash, ownership dilution, and personality-driven contributions.
- Competitors grow each month and compete for share, funding, talent, and AI leadership.
- Customer satisfaction affects reputation, churn, and several victory paths.
- World events temporarily change demand, churn, research velocity, and reputation.

## Player Tension

Good choices should rarely be obvious. A strong choice in one system should create pressure elsewhere:

- More research improves the future but burns runway.
- More investors reduce cash pressure but dilute founder control.
- More customers increase revenue but strain support capacity.
- More marketing grows the funnel but can worsen satisfaction if quality lags.
- More employees improve output but increase monthly burn.

## Customer Satisfaction

Customer satisfaction should never feel like a one-way punishment. It now has active and passive recovery paths:

- Product and Engineering staffing improve support capacity.
- Strong support capacity reduces churn pressure.
- Customer Success Sprint directly raises satisfaction and reduces churn risk.
- Some events reward customer-first decisions with satisfaction gains.
- Low satisfaction can still damage reputation and make the company harder to scale.

## Victory Routes

Current victory routes include:

- Market Dominance: high market share and serious revenue.
- AGI Company: unlock AGI and reach a major valuation.
- Bootstrapped Victory: strong profit with founder control and no active investors.
- Frontier Research Lab: release multiple AI models and unlock advanced technologies.
- IPO Victory: reach public-company scale while keeping meaningful founder ownership.
- Strategic Acquisition: build a loved, cash-rich company with a premium exit.
- Venture-Backed Unicorn: reach $1B valuation with venture support and revenue.
- Product Ecosystem: unlock the full product suite with massive adoption and high satisfaction.

## Tone

The tone should be futuristic, entrepreneurial, and sharp rather than cartoonish. Rogue Circuit styling should suggest electric dashboards, founder pressure, AI labs, investor memos, and industry headlines.
